KS Test

Explanation: The KS Test checks if a dataset follows a specific distribution by comparing its cumulative distribution to the expected one.

Define Hypotheses: Null hypothesis assumes the data follows the specified distribution; alternative suggests it does not.

Assumptions: Requires continuous data; the distribution of the data is not assumed to be normal.

Interpretation: A low p-value (< 0.05) indicates significant deviation from the specified distribution, rejecting the null hypothesis.

Formula: \( D_n = \sup_x |F_n(x) - F(x)| \)

Independent T-Test

Explanation: Compares the means of two independent groups to determine if they are significantly different.

Define Hypotheses: Null hypothesis assumes no difference in means; alternative suggests a significant difference.

Assumptions: Data should be normally distributed, with equal variances between the groups. Use Welch's T-Test if variances differ.

Interpretation: A low p-value (< 0.05) indicates a significant difference in means, rejecting the null hypothesis.

Formula: \( t = \frac{\bar{X}_1 - \bar{X}_2}{\sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}} \)

Mann-Whitney U Test

Explanation: A non-parametric test comparing distributions of two independent groups without assuming normality.

Define Hypotheses: Null hypothesis assumes no difference in distributions; alternative suggests a significant difference.

Assumptions: Data is ordinal, or continuous but not normally distributed, and samples are independent.

Interpretation: A low p-value (< 0.05) suggests a significant difference in distributions, rejecting the null hypothesis.

Formula: \( U = n_1 n_2 + \frac{n_1 (n_1 + 1)}{2} - R_1 \)

Confidence Interval

Explanation: A CI estimates a range likely to contain the true population parameter with a given confidence level.

Define Hypotheses: No null or alternative hypothesis; it evaluates the precision of the parameter.

Assumptions: Data should be representative, with larger samples increasing accuracy.

Interpretation: A 95% CI means we are confident the parameter lies within the interval 95% of the time.

Formula: \( CI = \bar{X} \pm Z \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \)
